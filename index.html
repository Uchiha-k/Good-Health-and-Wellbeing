Universal Health Coverage

One of the key targets of SDG 3 is to achieve universal health coverage, ensuring that everyone has access to essential health services without facing financial hardship.

Global Commitment

The international community has made a commitment to achieving SDG 3 by 2030 through concerted efforts at the national, regional, and global levels. Governments, civil society organizations, healthcare providers, and individuals all have a role to play in advancing this goal.

Features of SDG-3

(SDG 3) features several key aspects that highlight its importance in promoting global health and well-being. Here are some of the prominent features of SDG 3.

Health Equity and Access

One of the central tenets of SDG 3 is addressing health inequities and ensuring equitable access to health services for all, regardless of socioeconomic status, geographic location, gender, age, or other factors. This includes reaching vulnerable and marginalized populations who may face barriers to accessing healthcare.

  • Addressing Social Determinants: Achieving health equity requires addressing the underlying social determinants of health, such as income inequality, education, employment, housing, and access to clean water and sanitation. These social factors significantly influence individuals' health outcomes and access to healthcare services.
  • Reducing Health Disparities: Health equity efforts focus on reducing health disparities or inequalities in health outcomes between advantaged and disadvantaged groups. This may involve targeted interventions to address specific health challenges faced by marginalized or vulnerable populations, such as indigenous communities, refugees, persons with disabilities, and socio-economically disadvantaged groups.
  • Ensuring Fair Access to Healthcare: Health equity also entails ensuring fair access to healthcare services for all individuals and communities, regardless of their socio-economic status or geographic location. This includes eliminating barriers to access, such as financial constraints, lack of health insurance, transportation issues, language barriers, and discrimination.

Promotion of Mental Health and Well-being

SDG 3 recognizes the importance of mental health and well-being as integral components of overall health. It emphasizes the need for mental health promotion, prevention of mental disorders, and access to mental health services and support systems.

Promoting mental health involves creating supportive environments, strengthening coping skills, and fostering resilience to prevent mental health problems and enhance overall well-being. It encompasses a range of strategies and interventions aimed at individuals, families, communities, and societies to improve mental health outcomes and reduce the burden of mental illness.

Reduction of Mortality Rates

SDG 3 targets specific indicators related to reducing mortality rates, including maternal mortality, neonatal mortality, child mortality, and deaths from communicable and non-communicable diseases. Efforts to improve healthcare services, enhance disease prevention, and promote healthy behaviors contribute to achieving these targets.

Multi-Sectoral Collaboration

Achieving SDG 3 requires collaboration and partnerships across multiple sectors, including health, education, social welfare, environment, and economy. It involves engaging governments, civil society, the private sector, academia, and communities to work together towards common health goals.
