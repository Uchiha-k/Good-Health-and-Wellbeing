The Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria

The Global Fund is a partnership between governments, civil society, the private sector, and affected communities dedicated to accelerating the end of the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria. By mobilizing and investing resources, the Global Fund supports prevention, treatment, and care programs in countries with high burdens of these diseases. Through its innovative financing model and partnerships, the Global Fund has saved millions of lives and made significant progress towards ending these epidemics

Programs to tackle the three diseases registered a solid recovery after being disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic. After seeing the gains of the last two decades sharply reversed during 2020 and only a partial recovery the following year, in 2022 we put more people on antiretroviral treatment for HIV than ever before, we found and put on treatment more people with TB than ever before, and we distributed a record number of mosquito nets to prevent malaria.

The Global Fund Results Report 2023 details how our investments continued to deliver lifesaving services for people affected by HIV, TB and malaria. Our partnership also played a critical role in supporting countries and communities to accelerate their recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic and build resilient and sustainable systems for health that can respond to current and future health threats.

Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance

Gavi is a public-private partnership committed to increasing access to vaccines for children in low-income countries. By leveraging resources, negotiating vaccine prices, and supporting immunization programs, Gavi helps countries protect children from vaccine-preventable diseases and strengthen their healthcare systems. Since its inception, Gavi has helped immunize millions of children, prevent outbreaks, and build sustainable immunization programs worldwide.

As Part of its mission to save lives and protect people’s health by increasing equitable and sustainable use of vaccines,Gavi has helped vaccinate more than 1 billion children in 78 lower-income countries, preventing more than 17.3 million future deaths. Gavi’s impact draws on the strengths of its core partners, the World Health Organization, UNICEF, the World Bank and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, and plays a critical role in strengthening primary health care (PHC), bringing us closer to the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) of Universal Health Coverage (UHC), ensuring that no one is left behind.

Gavi also works with donors, including sovereign governments, private sector foundations and corporate partners; NGOs, advocacy groups, professional and community associations, faith-based organisations and academia; vaccine manufacturers, including those in emerging markets; research and technical health institutes; and implementing country governments.

World Health Organization (WHO) Global Health Initiatives

The World Health Organization leads several global health initiatives focused on addressing specific health challenges and promoting health equity worldwide. These initiatives include the Global Polio Eradication Initiative, the Stop TB Partnership, and the Roll Back Malaria Partnership, among others. By coordinating efforts, providing technical expertise, and mobilizing resources, WHO and its partners work to combat infectious diseases, improve maternal and child health, and strengthen health systems.

Every Woman Every Child

Launched by the United Nations Secretary-General, Every Woman Every Child is a global movement that aims to improve the health and wellbeing of women, children, and adolescents worldwide. By focusing on reducing maternal and child mortality, increasing access to essential healthcare services, and promoting gender equality in health, Every Woman Every Child seeks to achieve significant progress towards SDG 3 targets. Through advocacy, partnerships, and action, the movement mobilizes stakeholders to prioritize the health needs of women, children, and adolescents.
